TASK 5 – PREVENTION CHECKLIST
 Cyber Safety and UPI Security Guide
1. Create Strong and Distinct Passwords: Use complex passwords that combine uppercase and
lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid reusing passwords across different platforms. A
password manager can help securely store and manage credentials.
2. Enable Multi-Factor Authentication: Activate two-factor authentication (2FA) on critical accounts
such as banking, email, and social media. This adds an extra verification layer beyond just a
password.
3. Be Careful with Unknown Messages: Do not click on suspicious links or download attachments
from unknown senders. Cybercriminals often impersonate trusted entities to trick users into
revealing information.
4. Confirm Identity Before Sharing Data: Never share sensitive information like Aadhaar, PAN, or
passport details unless you are certain of the recipient’s authenticity. Always verify the source
independently.
5. Keep Systems Updated: Regularly update your devices and applications. Security updates fix
known vulnerabilities and reduce the risk of exploitation.
6. Protect UPI Credentials: Never disclose your UPI PIN, OTP, or card details. Legitimate
institutions will never request this information through calls or messages.
7. Use UPI Safely: Always verify the recipient’s name before approving payments. Avoid scanning
QR codes from untrusted sources and monitor transactions through SMS or email alerts.
8. Report Fraud Immediately: If you suspect fraud, report it without delay through official channels
such as the National Cyber Crime Portal or the helpline number 1930 to minimize losses.
Conclusion: Practicing these preventive measures can significantly reduce the risk of falling victim
to cyber fraud. Awareness, caution, and timely action are essential components of digital safety
